# Google Business Profile Optimization Guide

A fully optimized Google Business Profile (GBP) is essential for local search visibility. Follow this step-by-step guide to maximize your GBP’s potential.

## 1. Claim and Verify Your Profile
- Go to [google.com/business](https://www.google.com/business) and claim your listing.
- Verify via postcard, phone, email, or instant verification (if eligible).

## 2. Complete Every Section Thoroughly

### Business Information
- **Business Name**: Use exact real-world name (do **not** stuff keywords).
- **Address**: Enter precisely as it appears on your website and everywhere else.
- **Service Area**: If you serve customers at their location, define your service area (cities, zip codes, or radius).
- **Phone**: Use a local number (not a toll-free) if possible.
- **Website**: Link to your homepage or a dedicated location page.
- **Category**: Select the **primary category** that best describes your core business. Add up to 9 additional relevant categories.

### Hours of Operation
- Set regular hours accurately.
- Use **special hours** for holidays, closures, or temporary changes.

### Description
- Write a 750-character description highlighting what makes your business unique, key services, and your service area.
- Include relevant keywords naturally (e.g., "plumber in Austin").
- Avoid HTML, URLs, or promotional code language.

### Photos & Videos
- **Logo**: 250 x 250 px.
- **Cover Photo**: 1080 x 608 px (minimum).
- **Additional Photos**: Add at least 10 high-quality images of your storefront, team, work examples, and products.
- **Videos**: Short clips (30–60 seconds) showing your services or customer experience.
- Update photos regularly to keep the profile fresh.

## 3. Attributes & Services
- Select all applicable attributes (e.g., "Women-led," "Free Wi-Fi," "Outdoor seating").
- Add services and products with descriptions and prices (if available).

## 4. Posts & Updates
- Create regular posts (offers, events, updates, product highlights).
- Use high-quality images or videos.
- Include a clear call-to-action (e.g., "Learn more," "Call now").
- Aim for at least 1 post per week.

## 5. Q&A Section
- Preemptively add frequently asked questions and answer them yourself.
- Monitor and respond to user questions promptly.

## 6. Reviews
- Encourage customers to leave reviews (see `review-management-strategy.md`).
- Respond to **all** reviews – both positive and negative.
- Use responses to reinforce your brand voice and keywords (e.g., "Thank you for choosing our Austin plumbing team!").

## 7. Insights & Performance
- Check GBP Insights regularly to see how customers find you (search queries, calls, direction requests).
- Adjust your strategy based on data.

## 8. Maintain Consistency
- Ensure your NAP (Name, Address, Phone) matches exactly across your website, GBP, and all citations.
- Update any changes (hours, phone, address) immediately.

---

**Pro Tip**: Use the Google Business Profile app to manage your listing on the go and respond to reviews quickly.
