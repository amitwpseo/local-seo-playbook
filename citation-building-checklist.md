# Citation Building Checklist

Consistent citations (mentions of your business name, address, phone number) across the web are crucial for local SEO. Use this checklist to build and manage citations.

## Before You Start
- [ ] Standardize your NAP (Name, Address, Phone) exactly as it appears on your website and Google Business Profile.
- [ ] Decide on a consistent phone format (e.g., (512) 555-1234 or 512-555-1234).

## Top Tier Citation Sites (Always Include)
- [ ] Google Business Profile
- [ ] Apple Maps
- [ ] Bing Places
- [ ] Facebook Business
- [ ] Yelp
- [ ] Yellow Pages
- [ ] Superpages
- [ ] Foursquare
- [ ] MapQuest
- [ ] Better Business Bureau (if applicable)

## Industry-Specific Sites
Identify directories relevant to your niche. Examples:
- **Healthcare**: Healthgrades, Vitals, Zocdoc
- **Legal**: Avvo, Justia, FindLaw
- **Home Services**: Angi (Angie's List), HomeAdvisor, Houzz
- **Restaurants**: TripAdvisor, Zomato, OpenTable

## Local & Niche Directories
- [ ] Local chamber of commerce website
- [ ] Local business journals or news sites
- [ ] City/regional directories (e.g., "Austin Business Directory")
- [ ] Local community blogs or forums

## Citation Building Process
For each site:
1. [ ] Search if your business is already listed. If yes, claim it and update NAP to match.
2. [ ] If not listed, create a new account and submit your business.
3. [ ] Fill out all fields completely (description, hours, website, categories, photos).
4. [ ] Verify the listing if required (phone, email, postcard).
5. [ ] Double-check that NAP is **exactly** consistent with your standard format.

## Ongoing Maintenance
- [ ] Use a citation tracking tool (e.g., BrightLocal, Whitespark) to monitor new citations and duplicates.
- [ ] Quarterly audit: check top 10 citations for consistency.
- [ ] When you move or change phone number, update all citations immediately.

## Duplicate Listings
- [ ] Search for duplicates of your business name across directories.
- [ ] Claim duplicates and request removal or merge if possible.
- [ ] Keep a log of where duplicates exist and your progress in resolving them.

**Pro Tip**: Start with the most important sites first, then work your way down. Consistency matters more than quantity.
