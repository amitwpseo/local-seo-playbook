# Review Management Strategy

Online reviews influence local rankings and customer decisions. Implement this strategy to generate more reviews and handle them professionally.

## 1. Generating More Reviews

### Make It Easy
- Create a direct review link: `https://search.google.com/local/writereview?placeid=YOUR_PLACE_ID`
- Place the link in:
  - Email signatures
  - Follow-up emails after service
  - Text messages (SMS)
  - Website footer or "Review Us" page
  - Invoices or receipts

### Ask at the Right Moment
- Ask when customer satisfaction is highest (immediately after a successful service, when they express thanks).
- Train staff to mention: "If you're happy with our service, we'd love if you could leave a quick review."

### Incentivize (Ethically)
- You cannot offer a reward for positive reviews, but you can run a contest for *any* review (e.g., "Leave a review and enter to win a gift card"). Check platform guidelines.

## 2. Responding to Reviews

### Positive Reviews
- Always respond within 2-3 days.
- Personalize: mention something specific from their review.
- Express gratitude.
- Reinforce your brand keywords naturally (e.g., "We're thrilled to help with your plumbing needs in Austin!").

**Template:**
> Thank you so much, [Name]! We're delighted to hear that you enjoyed our [specific service]. Your kind words mean a lot to our team. We look forward to serving you again!

### Negative Reviews
- Respond promptly and professionally.
- Stay calm and empathetic.
- Acknowledge the issue and apologize.
- Offer to make it right offline (provide contact info).
- Do not argue publicly.

**Template:**
> Hi [Name], we're sorry to hear about your experience. This is not the standard we strive for. Please contact us at [email/phone] so we can understand what happened and find a solution. We appreciate your feedback.

## 3. Monitoring & Tracking
- Set up alerts for new reviews (Google Alerts, review monitoring tools).
- Use a spreadsheet to track:
  - Date of review
  - Platform
  - Rating
  - Response status
  - Any action taken

## 4. Leveraging Reviews on Your Website
- Embed a Google Reviews widget on your site (many plugins available).
- Showcase positive reviews on key landing pages.
- Add review snippets with schema markup for rich results.

## 5. Handling Fake or Malicious Reviews
- Flag inappropriate reviews on the platform (Google, Yelp) with explanation.
- Gather evidence (e.g., no record of customer) to support your case.
- Do not engage aggressively; maintain professionalism.

## 6. Integrating with CRM
- Automate review requests via CRM after a completed job.
- Tag customers who leave reviews for follow-up.

---

Remember: Consistent, authentic reviews build trust and improve local pack rankings.
